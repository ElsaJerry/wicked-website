Design Process

Ripley, the Casemaker

Three-Phase Approach

We followed a structured three-phase process to develop our advocacy solutions, moving from exploration to implementation.

Parallel Prototyping
3 Weeks

Explored 12 different concepts to identify the most promising direction. Converged on data collection through surveys and advocacy dashboards.

Iterative Development
Multiple Rounds

Built two workstreams: website redesign and revolutionary survey system. Progressed from wireframes to high-fidelity prototypes.

Refinement Phase
2 Weeks

Created functional prototypes and developed Ripley the Casemaker based on legislator feedback.

Key Prototypes

Phase Prototype Focus Area

Research Insights

Exploration Phase

Key Finding: Participants wanted simplicity and safety when sharing personal stories, especially given current political climate concerns.

Iterative Phase

Key Finding: Users needed clear impact messaging and comprehensive data overview before diving into specific details.

Final Insights

Key Finding: Solutions must empower advocates rather than replace them, providing transparency and systemic thinking.

Solution Development

Survey Design

Focused on transparency and clear data usage to build trust and encourage regular participation from WIC staff and participants.

Dashboard Creation

Emphasized autonomy and credibility, ensuring lobbyists maintain full control over data interpretation and storytelling.

Website Redesign

Created clear pathways for different stakeholder segments with specific call-to-actions throughout the user journey.

Final Implementation

During the refinement phase, we built functional prototypes using WordPress for the website and Streamlit for the dashboard. Legislator feedback inspired the creation of Ripley the Casemaker - an AI-powered advocacy assistant that transforms how advocates prepare for legislative meetings.
